Tuco2

Calculating your travel carbon footprint made effortless.

UX Case Study

Project Overview

The Tuco2 is an application that allows users to calculate their carbon footprint based on their transportation and accommodation choices. As part of the project team, my role was to redesign the application and create a new, fresh, and revamped look and feel that met the client's requirements and improved the overall user experience.

Responsibility

  • User research for needs
  • Wireframes and prototypes creation
  • Designing UX/UI
  • Collecting feedback
  • Making necessary changes
  • Collaborating with developers
  • Post-launch support and maintenance

Tools

  • Figma
  • Figjam
  • Photoshop
  • Illustrator

Problem Statement

The problem was the original Carbon Footprint Calculator had poor UI/UX, complicated navigation, outdated design, inadequate information, and limited functionality.

This resulted in user frustration, confusion, and incorrect carbon footprint calculations. The client was not satisfied with the application's performance and wanted a redesign to improve its interface, user experience, and overall design, while aligning with the client's branding.

Goals

The goals of the redesign were to improve the user interface, enhance the user experience, and create a more modern look and feel that aligned with the client's brand. The client also wanted to ensure that the application was user-friendly and easy to navigate, with clear instructions and information.

Design Process

The design process for redesigning the Tuco2 application began with research and analysis, which would involve conducting user research to identify the target audience, their needs, pain points, and goals, as well as analyzing the competition to understand best practices and industry standards.

Based on the research and analysis, the design and prototyping phase would involve creating wireframes, mockups, and prototypes to develop the new user interface and user experience. These designs would be tested with users to ensure usability and accessibility.

After the design and prototyping phase, the implementation and testing phase would begin. This would involve developing the application using the chosen technology and testing the application to ensure functionality, usability, and accessibility.

Feedback and refinement would be an ongoing process throughout the design process. Feedback from users and stakeholders would be collected and analyzed, and necessary changes would be made to improve the application's performance and usability.

Finally, after the application is launched, post-launch support and maintenance would be provided to ensure its continued success. This would include bug fixes, updates, and improvements based on user feedback and changing industry standards.

Other Screens

Target Audience

The Tuco2 application targets environmentally conscious individuals, travelers, businesses focused on sustainability, environmental organizations, non-profits promoting sustainable behavior, and government agencies monitoring carbon emissions.

Pain Points

1.

Poor UI/UX

Poor user interface and user experience, making it difficult for users to understand how to use the application

2.

Complicated Navigation

Complicated and confusing layout and navigation, leading to user frustration and abandonment.

3.

Outdated Design

Outdated design and look and feel that did not align with the client's branding or modern design trends.

4.

Inadequate Information

Inadequate information and instructions, leading to confusion and incorrect carbon footprint calculations.

5.

Limited Functionality

Limited functionality and features, making the application less useful and relevant for users.

Key Takeaways

The Tuco2 redesign project was a valuable experience for the UX team, resulting in several key takeaways. Thorough user research helped identify user needs and pain points, leading to a user-friendly and accessible application. Collaboration with the development team ensured functional and well-designed implementation. An iterative design process allowed for feedback and improvement throughout the project. Attention to accessibility and industry standards ensured a competitive and satisfactory user experience. These learnings emphasize the importance of user-centric design, collaboration, and attention to detail in future projects.

Conclusion

The conclusion emphasizes that the Carbon Footprint Calculator redesign project was a rewarding experience for the UX team. The team was able to demonstrate their expertise in user-centric design, collaboration, attention to detail, and adherence to industry standards. By conducting extensive user research and using an iterative design process, the team was able to create a functional, well-designed, and accessible application that met users' needs and remained competitive in the market. The insights and takeaways gained from this project will be invaluable for future projects.
